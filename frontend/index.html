    Model Architecture

    This dashboard uses a feedforward neural network (Multi-Layer Perceptron) trained to classify financial stress from a sequence of monthly financial records. The network is implemented in PyTorch with the following architecture:

    Input (9 features) ↓ Dense(128) + ReLU + Dropout ↓ Dense(64) + ReLU + Dropout ↓ Dense(1) + Sigmoid → probability 0–1

    The output is a probability between 0 and 1. Scores are converted to a 0–100 risk scale and bucketed into three categories based on calibrated thresholds.

    Stress Definition

    The model was trained to predict financial stress using the following definition, applied consistently during synthetic data generation and labeling:

    • Savings below 1 month of expenses at the end of the window, OR
    • 3 or more consecutive months of negative cash flow

    A profile is labeled stressed if either condition is true. Both conditions can occur simultaneously and reinforce each other.

    Risk Categories

    Category Score Range Interpretation
    Low < 35 Financial position appears stable. Low likelihood of stress indicators.
    Medium 35 – 64 Some risk factors present. Monitor savings and cash flow patterns.
    High ≥ 65 Multiple stress indicators detected. Review debt obligations and expense levels.

    The thresholds (35 and 65) are intentionally wider than the model's internal 0.5 decision boundary to communicate uncertainty — a score of 50 sits in the medium band rather than forcing a binary high/low split.

    Input Features (9 total)

    Raw monthly inputs are aggregated into 9 engineered features before being fed to the model. This is the feature engineering layer:

    • avg_income — Average monthly income across all submitted months
    • avg_expenses — Average monthly expenses across all submitted months (including debt payments)
    • final_savings — Cumulative savings at the end of the window (floored at 0)
    • expense_ratio — Average expenses divided by average income. Values near 1.0 indicate spending at income level.
    • credit_score — Credit score (300–850)
    • savings_months — Savings buffer measured in months of expenses. Higher = more resilient.
    • expense_volatility — Std dev ÷ mean of monthly expenses (coefficient of variation). Higher = more erratic spending.
    • net_cash_flow — Average monthly income minus average monthly expenses (debt is included in expenses)
    • savings_trend — Linear slope of cumulative savings over the window ($/month). Negative = declining savings.

    Training Data

    The model was trained on 3,000 synthetic financial profiles generated across four archetypes that reflect real-world income bands:

    • Struggling — Low income, expenses near or above income, minimal savings
    • Getting by — Moderate income, positive but thin cash flow
    • Stable — Comfortable income-to-expense ratio, some savings buffer
    • Comfortable — High income, strong savings, low debt ratio

    The synthetic generator calibrated archetype parameters to achieve approximately 35% stressed profiles, matching realistic population distributions. Each profile spans 12 months with 15% monthly variance on income and expenses.
